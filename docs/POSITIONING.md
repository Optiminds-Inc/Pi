# Pi — Positioning Brief

*Working draft · v0.1 · April 2026*

This document is the source of truth for how Pi describes itself, who it
serves, and what it stands for. Every page, screen, headline, and button
label should be traceable back to a paragraph here. If the product ships
something that contradicts this brief, the brief is wrong, or the work is.

---

## 1. What Pi is

**Pi is the AI marketing team for personal injury law firms.**

In one 30-minute call, Pi sets up every channel a modern PI firm needs to
acquire clients — Google Search Ads, Google Business Profile, Google Maps,
SEO, and AI Search citations. From the next morning, our agents run those
channels every day. The lawyer logs in for two minutes to see what shipped,
what's working, and where next month's clients are coming from.

Pi is not a SaaS tool, a dashboard, or a freelancer marketplace. It is a
team — built largely from AI agents — that delivers the same digital
marketing outcome the largest plaintiff firms get from $10K-a-month
agencies, priced like software, and entirely owned by the lawyer.

## 2. Who we serve

**Solo and small-to-mid-size personal injury law firms in the United States.**

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Size | 1 – 15 attorneys |
| Annual revenue | $1M – $30M |
| Decision maker | Managing partner / sole proprietor |
| Geography | One state or one metro |
| Existing marketing | Referrals, body-shop relationships, Yelp, an old website, perhaps a half-managed Google Ads account |
| Software stack | Google Workspace, a case-management system (Filevine, Clio, Litify), maybe a CRM |
| Sophistication | Knows that marketing matters; does not know which channels matter most or how to operate them |

These firms are not the target customer for Rankings.io ($10K/mo floor),
Scorpion ($60K+ annual contracts), or in-house marketing teams. They are
the 80% of PI firms who have been priced out of "real" digital marketing —
and have learned to live with referral randomness.

## 3. The problem we solve

### 3.1 The lawyer's reality

Most PI lawyers we've spoken with describe their growth this way:

> *"It's mostly referrals from past clients and the body shop down the road.
> A few cases from Yelp. I tried Google Ads once but I couldn't tell if it
> was working. The agency that pitched me wanted ten thousand a month and a
> twelve-month contract. I'm not sure what next month looks like."*

The throughline is **uncertainty**. Not lack of money, not lack of
ambition. **Lack of visibility into where clients come from, and lack of
control over making more of them come.**

### 3.2 The two old options

| | Agency (Rankings.io, Scorpion, etc.) | DIY tool / freelancer |
|---|---|---|
| Cost floor | $10,000+/month + $50K–$200K/yr | Low, but high time cost |
| Ramp | 0–3 month kickoff, 6–12 month ROI | You are the ramp |
| Visibility | Monthly PDFs and quarterly reviews | A dashboard you have to interpret |
| Control | Email the account manager | You run it |
| Account ownership | Often agency-controlled | Yours |
| Lock-in | Annual contracts the norm | None |

Neither option gives a small firm what they actually want: **the
sophistication of an enterprise marketing team, the price of software,
and the certainty of being in the driver's seat.**

### 3.3 The anxiety we name

The single feeling Pi exists to remove is the lawyer's **anxious
unfamiliarity** with their own client pipeline. Pi's promise is not "more
leads." Pi's promise is *"you will know — every week — where the next
client is coming from, and what to do if you want more."*

Every line of copy on the landing page, and every screen of the product,
should ladder back to that promise.

## 4. The product

### 4.1 The five channels Pi operates

| # | Channel | What it is | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Google Search Ads** | Paid keyword search on Google | Fastest channel to test, most controllable to scale, immediate signal |
| 2 | **Google Business Profile** | The card that appears when someone searches for the firm by name | First impression for ~70% of branded searches |
| 3 | **Google Maps** | Local pack + map listings | ~40% of intake calls now begin here |
| 4 | **SEO** | Articles + landing pages ranking on organic Google | Compounds: every page works for years |
| 5 | **AI Search** | Citations and structured content tuned for ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, etc. | Where prospects increasingly ask "find me a personal injury lawyer in [city]" |

The website is **infrastructure**, not a channel — it is where every
channel lands, so it must be polished, fast, and built to convert.

### 4.2 The Pi loop

Pi's user model is intentionally three steps long:

```
01 Strategy Call          02 Pi sets up + runs        03 You log in
   You + a Pi partner        Pi · daily                   You · two minutes a day
   30 minutes.               In your name. Forever.       See. Adjust. Move on.
```

**Step 1 — Strategy Call (30 minutes, with a Pi partner).**
We diagnose where the firm is today, agree on what we will set up, and
price every line. The output is a written plan and a fixed price. There
is no SDR layer, no slide deck, no "let me get back to you with a quote."

**Step 2 — Pi sets up, then Pi runs (Pi · every day).**
*Setup* (one-time, in week one): website polish or rebuild; Google
Business Profile; Google Maps; Ads, Analytics, Search Console accounts —
all in the lawyer's name; first 8 SEO + AI Search articles; call tracking
installed.
*Operation* (daily, ongoing): campaign reallocation against
cost-per-qualified-lead; new article publishing; GBP posts and review
replies; AI Search citation monitoring; lead capture and attribution.

**Step 3 — The lawyer logs in (the lawyer · two minutes a day).**
Three pages: **Home** (what shipped, what's working, who came in),
**Plan** (budget, focus, scope), **Leads** (every call and form). No
agent chat in the MVP — the user controls the system through visible,
opinionated UI: budget sliders, practice-area chips, content approval
toggles. We add chat later, when the agents are ready to be talked to.

### 4.3 What is one-time vs ongoing

| One-time (paid once, delivered in week 1–2) | Ongoing (the monthly platform) |
|---|---|
| Website rebuild or polish | Daily Google Ads management |
| Google Business Profile setup | 8–10 articles per month (SEO + AI Search) |
| Google Maps verification | GBP posts, photos, replied reviews |
| Account creation in lawyer's name | AI Search citation upkeep |
| First 8 SEO + AI Search articles | Lead tracking and attribution |
| Call tracking installation | Continuous A/B on landing pages |

### 4.4 What we do not do (yet)

- Demand letters, medical chronologies, case valuation. *(EvenUp territory.
  Convergence is on the roadmap.)*
- Litigation tooling, e-discovery, depositions.
- Local Services Ads beyond initial verification *(in the roadmap)*.
- Meta / TikTok / OTT video *(in the roadmap if a customer needs it)*.

## 5. Competitive frame

### 5.1 Versus the PI marketing agency (Rankings.io, Scorpion, etc.)

Pi delivers **the same outcome** — top of Google, top of Maps, signed
cases — at roughly one-third the cost, with same-week setup, full
account ownership, and live visibility. The agency's traditional moats
(specialist talent, relationships, dataset) are largely replicated by
PI-trained AI agents plus a small senior team. The agency's
non-replicable parts (in-person events, founder brand) are not what the
80% of PI firms actually buy on.

### 5.2 Versus DIY tools and freelancers

Pi is a team, not a tool. The lawyer does not write copy, build pages,
adjust bids, or wonder why a campaign stopped delivering. The lawyer
also does not have to manage a freelancer's hours, scope, or quality.

### 5.3 Versus EvenUp / Supio / case-work AI

Different layer of the funnel. EvenUp operates *after* a case is signed
(demand letters, medical chronologies, case valuation). Pi operates
*before* — getting the case in the door. We will converge with EvenUp's
territory over time, starting with intake automation. We are not
competing with EvenUp on day one.

### 5.4 The wedge sentence

> *"The same digital marketing outcome a $10,000-a-month PI agency would
> deliver — set up in one call, run by AI agents every day, every account
> in your name, priced like software."*

This is the single sentence we should be able to fall back on in any
sales conversation, voicemail, podcast, or LinkedIn post.

## 6. Core value pillars

These are the three pillars the landing page, the dashboard, and the
sales motion all build on. Numbered for reference.

### Pillar 1 — *Setup of every channel modern PI firms need*

We tell the lawyer which channels matter (most don't know), then we set
all of them up properly, in one motion, in the lawyer's name. The lawyer
walks away from the strategy call knowing the plan; two weeks later the
foundation is live. **Value to the lawyer:** they no longer have to
figure out which channel matters or how to operate it.

### Pillar 2 — *AI agents run, optimize, and report — daily*

After setup, Pi's agents operate every channel every day: budget moves
to the campaigns producing qualified leads, new articles ship on a
calendar, GBP posts publish, reviews get replied to, AI Search citations
get refreshed. **Value to the lawyer:** the equivalent of a full-time
marketing department, without hiring.

### Pillar 3 — *Two minutes a day, full visibility, full control*

The lawyer's only job is to log in, see what shipped, see what's
working, and adjust if they want. Budget is a slider. Practice areas are
chips. Content topics are approve / decline. No chat with the AI yet —
because we believe the UI should make 95% of decisions obvious without
asking. **Value to the lawyer:** certainty about the pipeline, without
becoming a marketer.

The product loop is not "AI does everything." It is **"AI does the work,
the lawyer keeps the steering wheel — and the steering wheel takes two
minutes a day."**

## 7. Pricing

### 7.1 The structure

Three lines on the bill. The same three lines, every month.

| Line | Amount | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| One-time foundation | $4,500 | Paid once at the strategy call |
| Monthly platform | $1,950 | Per month, month-to-month |
| Ad spend | $3,000 – $8,000 typical | Direct to Google, on the lawyer's card |

### 7.2 Why this works

- **No retainer model** — the lawyer is buying outcomes, not hours.
- **Ad spend direct to Google** — Pi never marks up media; the lawyer
  sees every dollar; if they leave Pi, the account stays with them.
- **No long-term contract** — we believe the work should justify itself
  monthly. If it doesn't, the lawyer leaves.
- **No offboarding fee** — every account is in the lawyer's name from
  day one, so there is nothing to "negotiate the return of."

### 7.3 The unit economics story (for partners and investors, not the lawyer)

Pi's COGS-per-customer at scale is dominated by the senior partner time
on the strategy call and a small fraction of agent compute / model
costs. Gross margins should resemble a vertical SaaS business at scale,
not a services business.

## 9. Voice and tone

### 9.1 Principles

| Principle | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Specific, not slogan** | Name the channel. State the cadence. Quote the price. Avoid "transform," "unlock," "revolutionize." |
| **Lawyer's perspective, not engineer's** | "The agents run your campaigns" is fine. "Multi-agent orchestration pipeline" is not. |
| **Trust through restraint** | Quiet confidence beats hype. We do not need exclamation points. |
| **Quiet certainty** | We say what is true. We do not promise outcomes we can't control (e.g., "10x leads"). We can promise *visibility*, *cadence*, *ownership*. |
| **Editorial weight, product clarity** | Page layout has the calm of a financial paper. Component density has the rigor of a piece of professional software. |

### 9.2 Words we use

- *Strategy Call*, *foundation*, *plan*, *leads*, *signed cases*, *cost
  per signed*, *channel*, *campaign*, *article*
- *In your name* (always — this is the ownership cue)
- *Live*, *visible*, *every day*
- *Pi*, *Pi runs*, *Pi sets up*

### 9.3 Words we avoid

- *Agent*, *workflow*, *pipeline*, *orchestration* — too engineering
- *Platform*, *suite*, *solution* — too SaaSy
- *AI-powered*, *next-generation*, *cutting-edge* — empty
- *Boost*, *transform*, *unlock*, *supercharge* — slogan
- *Synergy*, *holistic*, *robust* — corporate
- Any exclamation mark anywhere
- Any emoji anywhere

### 9.4 The sentence test

Before any line of copy ships, it has to pass three checks:

1. **Specific.** Could it appear in a 1990s *Wall Street Journal* article?
   If yes, it's specific enough.
2. **Plain.** Would a 55-year-old PI lawyer in Houston who barely uses
   software understand it on first read?
3. **Trust.** Does it overpromise? If a customer screenshots it and sends
   it to their lawyer (literal or figurative), is it fully defensible?

### 9.5 Number formatting

- Always tabular numerals.
- Currency: `$4,500`, `$2.1M` (compact at ≥ 1,000,000).
- Percent change: prefix `+` or `−` (en-dash for negative), never bare.
- Small magnitudes get exact figures; large ones get one decimal of
  significance (`$1.9K`, not `$1,883.41`).

## 10. Visual and interaction principles

### 10.1 Visual

- **Manrope at light weight** for display. **Optima** for the wordmark.
  Never bold the wordmark.
- **Pure white surface.** Paper-2 (`#fafafa`) for sidebar, table headers,
  subtle differentiation. No gradients, no shadows except a single
  hairline drop on hero preview.
- **Hairlines, not borders.** 1px lines at `#ececee`. Stronger
  (`#d2d2d7`) only where structural.
- **Numbers as heroes.** Display weight 300 on tabular numerals, large
  enough to anchor every card.
- **Color discipline.** Ink scale + a single near-black accent. Status
  colors are desaturated and used only for state.
- **No emoji, no AI shimmer, no gradients, no rounded-square illustration.**

### 10.2 Interaction

- Every clickable element has a hover state.
- Every change to plan or budget produces an immediate UI consequence
  (live impact projection, dirty marker, save/discard buttons in header).
- Every table row is hoverable and openable to a detail panel.
- Sticky page headers carry page identity, last-sync status, and primary
  action.
- The sidebar is persistent. The user always knows where they are.

### 10.3 Density

The product is dense, but never crowded. Every section answers one
question. Every card answers one sub-question. Every row carries one
fact. **Simplicity is the absence of clutter, not the absence of
substance.** A page with two huge headlines and three sentences is
*sparse*, not *simple*; we do not ship sparse.

## 11. Surface map (MVP)

### Public

- `/` Landing — value, channels, loop, pricing, CTA
- `/book` Strategy Call booking

### Authenticated (no auth in MVP — accessible via "See it live")

- `/home` Dashboard home — KPIs, eight-week trend, this week's activity,
  what's running, foundation
- `/plan` Plan — investment, ad budget + campaigns, focus areas + ZIPs,
  content cadence + topic queue, account ownership
- `/leads` Leads — funnel, filtered table, lead detail slide-over

### Sidebar groups

- **Workspace:** Home · Plan · Leads
- **Account:** Account ownership · Billing · Help

### Anti-surface

We deliberately do *not* show, in the MVP:

- Agent internals, prompts, model names
- Per-keyword bid logs
- An "all activity" infinite feed
- Settings pages with nine tabs
- A "marketplace" of any kind

## 12. What ships next (post-MVP)

In rough order:

1. **Agent chat** — the lawyer can talk to the marketing agent directly
   ("Push more into truck accidents," "Pause Slip & Fall for May," "Why
   did CPC jump on Tuesday?"). Designed to feel like talking to a senior
   marketer who has read every line of the dashboard.
2. **More channels** — Local Services Ads (verified), Meta / Instagram
   Ads, OTT video for higher-budget firms.
3. **Intake automation** — first move into EvenUp's territory: AI
   intake calls, follow-up sequences, qualification heuristics. Funnels
   directly into the existing Leads page.
4. **Case-value scoring** — for every qualified lead, an AI estimate of
   case value to help the lawyer prioritize.
5. **Demand letters and case-work** — the long convergence with
   post-intake AI tooling.

## 13. Internal definition of done — for any landing page or screen

A page is ready to ship when, on first read by a target lawyer, they can
finish all four sentences without prompting:

1. *"Pi is …"*
2. *"Pi is for me because …"*
3. *"To start, I would …"*
4. *"After I start, I would see …"*

If any of those is unclear, the page is not done.
